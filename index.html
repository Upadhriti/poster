Abstract
HIYA is an AI-powered wellness chatbot integrating 5,000-year-old Ayurvedic principles with modern nutrition science, mindfulness, music therapy, and aromatherapy. Built with MIT App Inventor and the Anthropic Claude API, HIYA classifies K–12 students into one of three Ayurvedic dosha types to deliver personalized, multi-modal wellness guidance within strict ethical safety protocols.

The Three Doshas

A 5-question quiz classifies each student into one of three Ayurvedic bioenergetic types for personalized guidance:

💨
Vata
Light energy, variable focus
↳ Warm nutrition + calming mindfulness
🔥
Pitta
Intense drive, strong focus
↳ Cooling diet + stress reduction
🌿
Kapha
Deep endurance, stability
↳ Energizing movement + light foods

Limitations

As a proof-of-concept, HIYA 1.0 has not undergone formal longitudinal evaluation or independent expert review. The constitutional quiz relies on self-reported data, which may not fully capture individual dosha complexity. Future iterations will address these gaps through structured user studies and certified Ayurvedic practitioner validation.

Conclusion

HIYA demonstrates significant potential as a model for integrating traditional holistic health frameworks with contemporary AI in K–12 education. By personalizing wellness guidance through Ayurvedic constitutional typing, HIYA addresses a meaningful gap in youth health literacy — showing how AI can preserve and transmit cultural wellness knowledge while enabling personalized, scalable, preventive health education for the next generation.
